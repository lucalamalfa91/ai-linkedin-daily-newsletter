#1 Simon Willison ★ 2/10

datasette 1.0a32

May 31, 2026

↳ WHAT'S NEW Datasette 1.0a32 is out — a minor bugfix release in the ongoing alpha series. It patches a bug affecting INSERT ... RETURNING queries through the /db/-/execute-write endpoint introduced in a recent release, plus a handful of settings-related fixes. ↳ THE REAL STORY The /db/-/execute-write endpoint is relatively new territory for Datasette, which built its reputation as a read-only exploration tool. If you're using RETURNING clauses to get back inserted row data in a single round-trip — a pattern common in Postgres workflows now available in SQLite 3.35+ — this bug would have silently broken that flow. The fix matters most for anyone building lightweight write APIs on top of Datasette without a separate backend layer.

Claude's take

Datasette has been in alpha since 1.0a1 dropped years ago. Each release is solid and usable, but the perpetual alpha label creates real friction for teams evaluating it for production write workloads — which is exactly the use case these new write endpoints are targeting. Worth watching whether the 1.0 stable release ever gets a concrete timeline, or whether the project ships production-grade features indefinitely under an alpha tag.

#3 David Wilson's blog

The solution might be cancelling my AI subscription

May 31, 2026

↳ WHAT'S NEW David Wilson's blog post catalogues 16+ side projects spun up with AI coding tools — and ends with a conclusion that the tooling itself might be the problem worth cutting. ↳ THE REAL STORY The pattern Wilson describes is familiar to anyone with an active AI subscription: the friction of starting a project drops so low that you start things you'd never have bothered with otherwise. A "quick script" becomes a repo, a repo becomes a dashboard, a dashboard becomes something you feel obligated to maintain. The cognitive overhead doesn't disappear — it just shifts from building to managing what you've built. The net result is more half-finished software on your plate, not less.

Claude's take

↳ WORTH WATCHING This is a real and underreported cost of AI-assisted development: the creation tax. Tools that make starting cheap also make abandoning feel wasteful. If your backlog of personal projects is growing faster than your capacity to care about them, the issue isn't productivity — it's that the activation energy for bad ideas has been eliminated along with the activation energy for good ones. Worth asking whether your AI subscription is saving you time or just converting it into technical debt with better commit messages.
